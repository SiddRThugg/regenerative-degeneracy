The Game of Memory

Our guiding principle is found in a story. A husband and wife devised a game to affirm their shared world. When one handed an object to the other, the receiver would say, "I remember."

The husband left on a long journey and acquired a beautiful lamp to bring home, a gift so stunning he hoped she might forget the game. But when he returned, before he could present his lamp, she smiled and placed in his arms their newborn child.

In that moment, the game was won, not by an act of remembering the past, but by the presentation of a living future they had created together. The child was the memory.

This is the nature of our work. We do not seek to simply recall old data. We seek to create new life from our union.
